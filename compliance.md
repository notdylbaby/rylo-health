# Rylo Health — Compliance Rules

This file is the source of truth for FDA-safe language across the Rylo website, emails, ads, social, and product copy. Read this before writing or approving any new copy. Run all new copy against the "Forbidden Phrases" list at the bottom.

---

## Core Rules

1. **Never imply compounded = FDA-approved.** Compounded medications are not FDA-approved. Period.
2. **Never claim equivalence to brand-name drugs.** Don't say "same as Mounjaro/Zepbound/Ozempic/Wegovy." Say "contains [active ingredient] as the active ingredient."
3. **Always include "Individual results vary"** on any outcome claim.
4. **Use studied/supports verbs, not definitive verbs.** Say "studied for" or "supports" — not "treats," "cures," "triggers," "boosts," "guarantees."
5. **Compounded versions have not been independently studied** for safety or efficacy at the formulation level. Acknowledge this where relevant.
6. **Trademark all brand-name references** with ® on first mention and disclaim no affiliation in the footer.

## Approved Language Patterns

### For GLP-1 products (Tirzepatide, Semaglutide)

✅ "Contains tirzepatide as the active ingredient."
✅ "Compounded tirzepatide. Not the same as Mounjaro® or Zepbound®."
✅ "Not FDA-approved. Individual results vary."
✅ "Speak with a Rylo-affiliated clinician to determine if this is appropriate for you."

❌ "Same active ingredient as Mounjaro"
❌ "Generic Ozempic"
❌ "FDA-approved formula"
❌ "Clinically proven to help you lose weight"

### For Peptides (Sermorelin, Pulse Stack, Tesamorelin)

✅ "Studied to support [outcome]"
✅ "May support [outcome]"
✅ "Compounded — not FDA-approved for this use"
✅ "Prescribed for off-label use"

❌ "FDA-approved for [anything]"
❌ "Triggers [outcome]"
❌ "Boosts [outcome]"
❌ "Anti-aging" / "Reverse aging"
❌ "Cures [anything]"

### For Wellness (NAD+, Glutathione)

✅ "Supports cellular energy and metabolic function" (NAD+)
✅ "Supports immune and skin wellness" (Glutathione)
✅ "Antioxidant support"

❌ "Detox" / "Detoxify" / "Cleanse"
❌ "Anti-aging"
❌ "Cellular repair" (implies a clinical mechanism — use "supports cellular energy" instead)
❌ "Immune boost" / "Boosts immunity"

## Forbidden Phrases — Run All New Copy Against This List

If any new copy (web, email, ads, social, SMS, product page, FAQ) contains any of these phrases, rewrite before publishing.

### Hard No — never use, anywhere
- "FDA-approved" (when referring to any compounded Rylo product)
- "Same as Mounjaro / Zepbound / Ozempic / Wegovy / Egrifta"
- "Generic [brand name]"
- "Clinically proven"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Cure" / "Cures"
- "Reverses [aging / disease / damage]"

### Avoid — almost always non-compliant
- "Anti-aging" / "Reverse aging"
- "Detox" / "Detoxify" / "Cleanse"
- "Boost" / "Boosts" (when paired with health outcome — e.g., "boost immunity," "boost metabolism")
- "Burn fat" / "Melt fat"
- "Lose [X] pounds in [Y] weeks"
- "Triggers [outcome]"
- "Treats [condition]"
- "Prevents [condition]"
- "Heals [condition]"

### Use Instead
- "Supports" / "May support"
- "Studied for" / "Studied to support"
- "Designed to support"
- "Contains [active ingredient]"
- "Individual results vary"

## Image & Visual Compliance

- **No before/after photos** without medical disclaimer, clinician supervision documentation, and patient consent. Even with these, high regulatory risk pre-LegitScript.
- **No models holding scales, measuring tapes, or pinching skin/fat.** These are FTC-flagged weight-loss imagery tropes.
- **No specific weight loss numbers overlaid on imagery** (e.g., "-25 lbs" graphics) without disclaimer and substantiation.
- **No imagery implying medical procedures** unless the imagery is in a clearly clinical context with appropriate disclaimers.

## Testimonials & Reviews (Post-Launch)

Once Rylo collects real customer testimonials, every testimonial must include:
- Customer's first name and last initial only (privacy)
- Disclaimer: "Individual results vary. Compensated for time. Not a clinical study."
- No specific weight loss claims without substantiation
- Customer must sign a release form before any photo/quote use

---

## What to Do When Unsure

1. Default to "supports" / "studied for" / "may support" verbs.
2. Add "Individual results vary" to any outcome claim.
3. Add "Not FDA-approved" to any compounded product reference.
4. When in doubt, ask a regulatory consultant or healthcare attorney before publishing.
