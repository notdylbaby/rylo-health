Glutathione injections are compounded medications and are not FDA-approved. A licensed provider will determine if this treatment is appropriate for you. Individual results may vary. Prescription required.

Glutathione is the body's most powerful antioxidant, produced naturally in every cell. It neutralizes free radicals, supports liver detoxification, and plays a critical role in immune function and skin health. Levels decline with age, stress, and poor nutrition — making replenishment especially valuable as you get older.

Patients commonly report brighter, more even skin tone, improved energy, reduced oxidative stress, better liver function, and stronger immune resilience. Glutathione is also popular for its skin-brightening effects, as it inhibits melanin production and promotes a more luminous complexion.

Glutathione is administered as a subcutaneous injection — a small injection under the skin, typically in the abdomen or thigh. Your provider will guide you on dosing frequency and technique. Most patients inject 2–3 times per week depending on their treatment plan.

Many patients notice improved energy and a subtle glow within the first 2–4 weeks. More significant skin brightening and wellness improvements typically develop over 6–12 weeks of consistent use. Results vary based on your starting glutathione levels, lifestyle, and dosing protocol.

Glutathione injections are generally well-tolerated. Some patients experience mild discomfort at the injection site or temporary bloating — usually resolving quickly. Your provider will review your health history and adjust your protocol as needed.

Oral glutathione is largely broken down in your digestive system before it reaches your cells — research suggests less than 20% is typically absorbed. Subcutaneous injections bypass digestion entirely, delivering glutathione directly into your bloodstream for significantly higher bioavailability and more consistent results.
